Part Two · Vocation

More Than a Career

A career asks, What work will I do? Vocation asks a deeper question: How am I called to love, serve, and become holy?

You do not need to know your future vocation right now. But you can begin noticing the clues: what you care about, what gifts you have, what needs you see around you, and what kind of person you are becoming through your choices.

Part Three · Love

What Is Love, Really?

People use the word love constantly, but they do not always mean the same thing.

In this unit, you will compare different views of love. A secular view may focus on emotion, attraction, pleasure, biology, or social connection. A Catholic view asks whether love is also a choice to seek the good of another person.

Part Four · Relationships

Love Has to Become Responsible

Relationships can reveal our best qualities, but they can also reveal jealousy, selfishness, fear, pressure, and immaturity.

In this unit, you will look at dating, infatuation, love, sexuality, chastity, and Theology of the Body. The goal is not to embarrass anyone. The goal is to ask what it means to treat another person with dignity.

Scenario 1

Someone constantly checks their partner's messages because they say, "I only do it because I love you."

This points toward control and distrust, not mature love. Real love respects dignity and freedom.
Scenario 2

Two friends disagree, but they listen carefully, apologize when needed, and try to understand each other.

This shows respect, honesty, and patience. These are signs of mature relationships.
Scenario 3

A person pressures someone to prove love by doing something they are not comfortable with.

Pressure is not love. Catholic teaching connects love with freedom, dignity, and responsibility.
